I am a postdoctoral researcher at the Planetary Plasma Atmosphere Research Center (PPARC) at Tohoku University . My research focuses on the study of magnetospheres using radio and plasma measurements. More specifically, my current position aims at preparing ESA/JUICE and ESA-JAXA/Bepi Columbo radio instruments for the exploration of Jupiter’s moons and magnetosphere and Mercury’s magnetosphere.

Prior to joining PPARC, I obtained my PhD (2025) in Marseille, France, at the Laboratoire d’Astrophysique de Marseille (AMU). My thesis, “Understanding Jovian radio auroral emissions with Juno”, was done with Laurent Lamy and in collaboration with LIRA, Observatoire de Paris. During this work, I analyzed in situ radio, plasma, and magnetic measurements from NASA/Juno to identify and characterize Jovian radio auroral sources. This resulted in XX publications (4 first author publications see XX) in which, thanks to my new methods, I confirmed the generation mechanism of these emissions, identified the sources of energy of each component of the Jovian radio auroral spectrum, and discovered harmonics.

My Bachelor’s degree was also obtained at Paris Saclay University (2020).
